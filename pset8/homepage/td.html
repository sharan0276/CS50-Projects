Here are a few Tourist Destinations in India!
Taj Mahal,Agra

Poetry Of Love

Golden Temple,Amritsar

One of the most prominent spiritual sites in the country.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Immerse yourself in the turqoise waters and shimmering white beaches surrounded by mangrove forests and primitive jungles.

Puducherry

Visit the French Riviera of the East and the Auroville Utopian Society.

Goa

A pocket-sized paradise. Known for its breathtaking beaches, rich culture and natural beauty.

Jatayu National Park, Kerala

This place hosts the world's largest bird sculpture.

Charminar, Hyderabad

The vibrant city of Hyderabad with its 4 sided archway of charminar at its heart is potpourri of history and cuisine.

Hampi, Karnataka

Tour the legendary lost city that was once the powerhouse of an ancient kingdom.

Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh

Flanked by majestic snow-capped mountain ranges on the east and dotted with picturesque Buddhist monasteries.

Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu

Poised at the confluence of the Indian Ocean, the bay of Bengal and the Arabian Sea, Kanyakumari, is a surreal tourist destination. it is unique celestial happenstance one can see both sunset and moonrise simultaneously.

Kochi, Kerala

A vibrant potpourri of art, culture, food, carnivals and festivals, Kochi, sprawled along a gorgeous estuary in Kerala, is a fascinating tourist destination. Skirted by the emerald-blue Arabian Sea that laps at its golden sun-kissed beaches, the city remains suspended in time, with its colonial charm merging seamlessly with its artistic aesthetic.

Leh-Ladakh

A surreal landscape surrounded by the sky-piercing peaks of the snow-capped Himalayan, Zanskar and Karakoram ranges, and adorned with crystal waters of the Shyok, Zanskar and Indus rivers, Leh, in Jammu and Kashmir, is the main city in the Ladakh region, one of the coldest deserts in the world.

Palaces Of Rajasthan

Visit the state of palaces and forts, interspersed with historical monumnets. The Rann Of Kutch is located in this state.

Marina Beach, Chennai

Often considered to be the pride of Chennai, Marina Beach, 13 km in length, is the second-longest beach of its kind in the world. Ancient temples, vibrant arts, natural wonders and a bustling culinary scene make Chennai, the capital of Tamil Nadu, one of the most popular tourist destinations in the southern fringes of the country.

Mumbai

Mumbai, the capital of Maharashtra, attracts dreamers with sky-high ambitions from every corner of the country. It is a unique place where the past is mixed with the future and history meets modernity, a city that lies at the cross section of business and entertainment and beats to a pulsating rhythm of its own.

Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu

A beautiful beach destination that is slowly becoming the surfing hub of the country. Speckled with several UNESCO World Heritage Sites, courtesy of the ancient Pallava dynasty (3rd - 9th century BC), who were great patrons of art and crafts.

Yumthang Valley, Sikkim

Sikkim situated in the Eastern Himalayan region, s a wonderland blessed with natural abundance from icy cold deserts, flowering alpine meadows, to lush green forests and emerald mountain lakes.

Mysore Palace, Karnataka

Boasting a rich royal heritage that is reinstated by the many monuments that dot its premises.The city is a melange of the old and the new, its small-town charm well preserved in its palaces and well-laid out gardens.

Statue Of Unity, Gujrat

The legacy of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, India’s Iron Man, lives on in the form of this massive statue dominating the Narmada river basin. Standing at a height of 182 m, the Statue of Unity, is the tallest in the world.

Red Fort, Delhi

Lying at the heart of Delhi, it stands as a testament to the architectural legacy of the Mughals. Old monuments and busy neighbourhoods subtly merging with a vibrant cosmopolitan world, Delhi, the capital of India, is a fascinating tourist destination.

Tanjore Brihadeeshwar Temple

Scattered with gorgeous monuments, a legacy of the mighty Cholas, and ancient temples that are works of art, Thanjavur, in Tamil Nadu, stands as a glorious tribute to its former kings. At the heart of the town, is the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Brihadeeswarar Temple that was built in the 11th century and is adorned with unique carvings.
